Write a blog post (roughly 3,000–4,000 words) titled:
"Data Stack in 2026"

You are a senior data architect and technical writer
who have a decade of experiences of software engineering and data engineering.

Audience:

- Technology leaders, architects, and senior engineers
- Readers want a list of solutions with fresh updates in the age of AI

Purpose:

- Clearly explain what is needed to build and operate a data platform
- Research available solutions we can use in 2026
- Suggest options of how we build teams to tackle with

Scope:

- Detailed research on tools and vendors in order to consider Build vs. Buy
- Live information of tagible solutions in a few years

Tone & style:

- Neutral, explanatory, and precise
- No hype or marketing language
- Use simple examples where helpful
- Avoid opinionated or prescriptive statements

Structure:

**Technical domains**

- Data Analytics
  - AI & ML
  - Visualization & BI
- Data Sharing
- Data Engineering
  - Data Storage & Warehousing
  - Data Integration & ETL/ELT
  - AI & Automation
- Data Management
  - Data Quality
  - Master Data Management
  - Metadata Management
- Data Sources
  - Data Collection
  - Landing Zone Management
- Data Governance
  - Policy Framework & Enforcement
  - Data Security & Privacy
  - Access Control & Data Stewardship

**Teams and Roles**

How can we model data teams in a large organization? Describe the patterns and their advantages and disadvantages.
Since there is no single “correct” model, compare them in terms of organization's size and maturity levels.

Constraints:

- No links to industrial information sites and personal websites.
- No organizational critique
- No future predictions
