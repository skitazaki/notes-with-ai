# Image Generation Prompt - Ownership Boundaries

Create a publication-quality architecture illustration titled:

"Ownership Boundaries"

Purpose:

Show how responsibility for change and operation can be divided across the same system.
The image should make ownership visible without confusing it with structural layers or deployment units.

Style:

- clean organizational-technical hybrid diagram
- vector-style blocks and overlays
- light background
- clear grouped regions with labels
- 16:9 aspect ratio

Composition:

Show one shared platform and product system.
Overlay ownership zones for several responsible groups such as:

- Product Domain Team
- Platform Team
- Security or Governance Team
- Data Team
- Shared Services

Use boundary lines, shaded zones, or responsibility overlays to show that ownership cuts across technical components in selective ways.
Add a few callouts for:

- who changes this
- who operates this
- who defines the contract
- who handles incidents

Central message:

Ownership boundaries describe responsibility for change and operation.
They may align with technical boundaries, but they are not the same thing.

Do:

- show overlapping or adjacent responsibilities carefully
- keep the example credible for modern platform engineering
- emphasize accountability and coordination points

Do not:

- turn the image into a formal org chart
- assume every service belongs cleanly to one team
- use decorative business icons
