# Image Generation Prompt - Decision Frameworks

Create a documentation-quality process infographic titled:

"Decision Frameworks"

Purpose:

Show how architecture concepts support better decisions by connecting the concern, the relevant dimension, the tradeoffs, the view, and the final choice.

Style:

- decision-oriented technical infographic
- clean vector process design
- light background
- professional editorial look
- strong information hierarchy
- 16:9 aspect ratio

Composition:

Create a left-to-right decision flow with 5 major stages:

- Concern
- Relevant Dimension
- Options and Tradeoffs
- View or Evidence
- Decision

Use a concrete but generic architecture question, such as policy enforcement placement or control-plane versus data-plane separation.
Include compact side labels for:

- stakeholder
- constraint
- risk
- reversibility
- evidence needed

Show that different dimensions may be selected depending on the concern, for example structural, operational, strategic, ownership, or communication.

Central message:

Architecture concepts are useful when they improve judgment.
Choose the reasoning model that matches the decision.

Do:

- make the progression easy to follow
- keep the example reusable across architecture topics
- emphasize decision quality over process ceremony

Do not:

- turn the figure into an ADR template screenshot
- overload the image with text
- imply that one framework fits every decision
